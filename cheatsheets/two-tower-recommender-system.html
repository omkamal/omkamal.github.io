The Two-Tower Recommender

Matching the right article to the right agency

A visual, plain-language tour of how two neural "towers" learn to predict whether a story belongs with an agency — scored from 0 to 1.

Article features Agency profile Collaborative filtering Match score
The Challenge

Too many stories, too many agencies

Hand-matching content to the right outlet is slow, inconsistent, and impossible at scale.

📰

Thousands of articles

New stories arrive every hour across every topic and region.

🏢

Hundreds of agencies

Each has its own focus, audience, region and appetite.

🐌

Manual & slow

Editors can't review every possible pairing by hand.

🎯

The goal

Auto-score every (article, agency) pair from 0 to 1.

We want a model that, for any story and any agency, instantly answers: "How likely is this a good match?"

The Big Idea — In Plain Words

Think of it as a matchmaker

📝

1. Two profiles

The article describes itself; the agency describes itself.

🔢

2. Two fingerprints

The model turns each profile into a compact list of numbers.

⚖️

3. Compare

It checks how well the two fingerprints fit together.

💯

4. A match score

Out comes a number from 0 (no) to 1 (perfect fit).

Just like a dating app compares two people, the two-tower model compares a story and an agency — and predicts the chance they belong together.

First, a Key Concept

What is an embedding?

A way to turn words and features into coordinates on a map, where similar things sit close together.

🗺️

Meaning becomes location

"Human rights in Brazil" lands near "Politics in LATAM" — far from "Pacific Rim chips".

📐

A list of numbers

Each item becomes a vector, e.g. [0.21, -0.88, 0.04, …] — its fingerprint.

🤝

Closeness = relevance

If two fingerprints are near each other, the items are related.

An embedding tower is simply the part of the model that produces these fingerprints.

The Architecture

Two towers, built independently

Each tower is its own small neural network. They never share inputs — they only meet at the very top.

The Scoring Head

Where the towers meet

Join the two fingerprints, pass them through a fully-connected network, and squeeze the result into a 0–1 score.

Classic two-tower setups compare fingerprints with a simple dot-product for ultra-fast retrieval; adding this dense "interaction head" trades a little speed for sharper ranking accuracy.

Going Further

Adding collaborative filtering

Content features describe what things are. Collaborative filtering learns from who interacted with what.

🆔

ID embeddings (hybrid)

Give every agency & article a learned ID vector, concatenated with the content fingerprint — capturing behavior the words don't.

📚

History pooling

Average the embeddings of articles an agency previously accepted to build a "taste vector".

🕸️

Graph / GNN signal

Model the agency–article interaction graph (e.g. LightGCN) and learn embeddings from the connections themselves.

❄️

Solves cold-start

Content handles brand-new items; collaborative signal sharpens matches for familiar ones. Best of both.

The winning recipe is usually hybrid: content + collaborative in the same two towers.

How It Learns

Teaching the model from past matches

✅

Positive & negative pairs

Pairs that were accepted → label 1. Pairs ignored or never shown (sampled) → label 0.

📉

Binary cross-entropy

The model is penalised whenever its predicted score is far from the true 0/1 label.

🔁

Trained jointly

Both towers and the scoring head learn together via gradient descent, sharpening every fingerprint.

Over millions of historical article–agency interactions, the towers learn to place good matches close together and bad matches far apart.

In a Nutshell

Key takeaways

1

Two independent towers turn an article and an agency into comparable numeric fingerprints (embeddings).

2

A fully-connected head with a sigmoid joins the fingerprints and outputs a 0–1 match score.

3

Mixing content features with collaborative filtering captures both meaning and behavior — and beats cold-start.

4

Pre-computed agency fingerprints make it fast, scalable, and self-improving in production.

The right story reaches the right agency — automatically, and at scale.
